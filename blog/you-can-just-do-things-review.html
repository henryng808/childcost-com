You Can Just Do Things: The Blueprint for Raising High-Agency Kids

ChildCost Editorial Team
July 19, 2026 • 10 min read

Published June 2, 2026 by HarperCollins, You Can Just Do Things: How High-Agency People Get What They Want Out of Life by Cate Hall and Sasha Chapin is the book that every parent raising children in the 2020s needs. Hall argues that the so-called "rules" of success aren't rules at all — they're productivity guidelines for low-agency people. For parents, this is a revelation: agency is not innate; it's taught.

The Agency Dividend

Hall and Chapin identify the "agency gap" — the difference between children who are taught to wait for permission and those who are taught to build. In a 2026 economy characterized by rapid AI disruption, You Can Just Do Things provides the tactical framework for raising "high-agency" children: kids who increase their surface area for luck, embrace asymmetric bets, and understand that the path to achievement is through action, not permission.

Core Principles for Parents:

  • Surface Area for Luck: Teaching children to position themselves where opportunity can find them.
  • Embracing Asymmetric Bets: The parenting strategy of allowing low-cost, high-upside risks.
  • Permissionless Action: Fostering a mindset where children see problems as invitations, not barriers.

The ChildCost Verdict

You Can Just Do Things is the essential parenting playbook for the post-AI era. Hall and Chapin give us the language to describe the most important skill a child can possess: the belief that their actions shape their world.
