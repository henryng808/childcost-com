Everything All at Once: A Widow's Guide to Holding It Together

ChildCost Editorial Team
July 22, 2026 • 10 min read

Steph Catudal's Everything All at Once: A Memoir is the book that every grieving family needs — and that every friend of a grieving family should read. When Catudal lost her husband to cancer, she found herself navigating not only her own devastation but the impossible task of holding space for her children's grief while drowning in her own. This memoir is the story of how she survived — with the help of community, radical honesty, and the slow, patient work of rebuilding.

The Community Lifeline

Catudal identifies the "care circle" — the network of friends, neighbors, and community members who step in when the nuclear family structure fails. In a 2026 world where isolation and loneliness are public health crises, Everything All at Once is a masterclass in accepting help and building the infrastructure of mutual care. Featured on the Moms Don't Have Time to Read Books podcast, Catudal's voice is raw, funny, and fiercely honest.

Lessons for Families Facing Loss:

  • Radical Honesty with Children: How to grieve truthfully while protecting a child's sense of security.
  • The Care Circle: Practical strategies for building and accepting community support during crisis.
  • Resilience After Loss: The long, non-linear journey of rebuilding a family identity after profound loss.

The ChildCost Verdict

Everything All at Once is a luminous, deeply practical memoir about the hardest thing a family can face. Catudal proves that community is not a luxury — it is the infrastructure that holds families together when everything else falls apart.
