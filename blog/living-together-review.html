Living, Together: Reclaiming the Multi-Generational Home

ChildCost Editorial Team
July 6, 2026 • 11 min read

Released on July 1, 2026, Living, Together by Samantha Paige Rosen is a critical look at the "Great Re-Convergence" of the modern family. Rosen explores the emotional and economic ROI of multigenerational living, arguing that the traditional nuclear family model is an anomaly in human history.

The Economics of Shared Care

Rosen identifies the "care dividend"—the massive financial and emotional benefit of having grandparents or extended community involved in daily child-rearing. In an era where childcare costs in the US have hit a record $32,000 annually, Living, Together provides a tactical blueprint for families looking to build a resilient, cost-shared ecosystem. Her focus on "found family" and mutual aid groups offers a roadmap for those without traditional ancestral roots.

Core Strategies for 2026:

  • Relational Sovereignty: Setting boundaries that allow for privacy within a shared household.
  • Resource Pooling: How shared logistics reduce the individual family burden and build collective wealth.
  • Cognitive Legacy: The value of children learning grit and history from older generations.

The ChildCost Verdict

Living, Together is the definitive guide to the "New Normal" of the late 2020s. Rosen gives parents the tools and the courage to rethink the walls around their homes and build a family thread that is stronger because it is shared.
