Like, Follow, Subscribe: The True Cost of Influencer Childhoods

ChildCost Editorial Team

In the high-intensity digital economy of 2026, a new parenting crisis has emerged: the monetization of childhood. Like, Follow, Subscribe: Influencer Kids and the Cost of a Childhood Online provides a hard-hitting investigation into the economic and emotional liabilities of raising children in the public eye of social media.

The Economics of Exploitation

The book explores the "digital labor" performed by children in "sharenting" households. While the financial returns can be massive, the author details the long-term emotional and legal costs—from privacy erosion to the breakdown of parent-child trust. In an era where a child's digital footprint is permanent, this book argues that "privacy is the ultimate developmental capital."

Strategic Pillars for 2026:

  • The Privacy Audit: Identifying the hidden risks in your current social media habits.
  • Co-Intelligence & Consent: Teaching children digital agency and the right to their own likeness.
  • The Future Liability: Understanding the long-term social and career implications of a forced digital legacy.
