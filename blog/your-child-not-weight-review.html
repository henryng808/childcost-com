Your Child is Not Their Weight: Parenting with Empathy

ChildCost Editorial Team
June 14, 2026 • 12 min read

In a world obsessed with measurements and metrics, Joey Skelton’s 2026 release, Your Child is Not Their Weight, published by the American Academy of Pediatrics, is a radical call for empathy. Skelton argues that our focus on weight often masks deeper needs and can inadvertently damage a child's relationship with their body and their parents.

Moving Beyond the Scale

Skelton provides a science-backed framework for fostering a healthy home environment that prioritizes overall wellbeing over BMI. He emphasizes the importance of language, shared family meals, and modeling a positive relationship with food and movement. In 2026, where digital comparison is constant, this book is a vital shield for your child's self-esteem.

Key Takeaways for 2026:

  • Language Matters: How to discuss health and bodies without shame or judgment.
  • Holistic Health: Focusing on sleep, stress management, and emotional regulation.
  • The Comparison Trap: Strategies to help children navigate a size-obsessed digital world.
