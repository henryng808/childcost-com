The Hideaway: Shea Ernshaw on Reclaiming Private Spaces

ChildCost Editorial Team
July 5, 2026 • 10 min read

Released in July 2026, The Hideaway by Shea Ernshaw is a haunting meditation on the importance of private childhood spaces. Ernshaw explores the deep, internal world of a child seeking refuge from a world that demands constant visibility and performance.

The Architecture of Agency

Ernshaw identifies the "visibility liability"—the cost of growing up in a world where every milestone is tracked and shared. In an era of pervasive digital oversight, The Hideaway acts as a survival manual for protecting a child's cognitive sovereignty. The book emphasizes that true grit and resilience are built in the quiet, unobserved moments of childhood.

Core Principles for Parents:

  • Sovereign Zones: Creating physical and digital "hideaways" where children can explore without judgment.
  • Algorithmic Erasure: The value of opting out of performative parenting to protect child agency.
  • Internal Grit: Fostering the self-directed learning that only happens in private exploration.

The ChildCost Verdict

The Hideaway is a must-read for parents navigating the late 2020s. Ernshaw gives us the language to describe the intangible value of privacy and the essential work of building a home that protects childhood agency.
