I'd Like to Play Alone, Please: The Comedy of Survival

ChildCost Editorial Team
June 8, 2026 • 8 min read

Sometimes, the only way to get through the 18-year investment of parenting is to laugh at the absurdity of it all. In I'd Like to Play Alone, Please, comedian Tom Segura offers a brutally honest, often hilarious look at the contradictions and chaos of modern family life in 2026.

The Unfiltered Truth

Segura doesn't sugarcoat the experience. He dives into the sleep deprivation, the financial stress of "childcare cliffs," and the bizarre social pressures of the parenting community. It’s a refreshing antidote to the "perfect parent" narratives that dominate social media.

Takeaways for the Tired Parent:

  • The Gift of Irreverence: Learning to let go of perfectionism through humor.
  • Navigating the Chaos: Relatable stories about the daily grind of raising kids in a tech-saturated world.
  • Community through Comedy: A reminder that we are all struggling in the same, often hilarious, ways.

The ChildCost Verdict

I'd Like to Play Alone, Please is the book you read when you've had a long day and need to remember that you're not the only one who feels like they're making it up as they go along. It's funny, sharp, and surprisingly therapeutic.
