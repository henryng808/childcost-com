Cribsheet: Better Decisions Through Data

ChildCost Editorial Team
May 18, 2026 • 7 min read

The Parenting Pain Point

Parenting advice is often contradictory, judgmental, and based on "tradition" rather than evidence. For the thinking parent, this creates a state of decision paralysis. Should you sleep train? Is breastfeeding truly mandatory for IQ? When is the right time for childcare? The lack of clear data leads to unnecessary guilt and expensive mistakes.

The Solution: The Economist's View

Emily Oster, an economics professor at Brown University, applies the rigors of data science to early childhood. In **Cribsheet**, she parses the best available studies to give parents the "bottom line" on the big decisions from birth to preschool.

  • Childcare Choices: A deep dive into the data on nannies vs. daycare, highlighting that there is no one-size-fits-all "correct" answer.
  • Sleep Training: Debunking myths about long-term trauma and focusing on what actually helps family well-being.
  • The Decision Framework: Oster teaches you *how* to think about decisions, weighing costs and benefits for your specific family unit.

The ChildCost ROI

Time is the ultimate currency of parenting. **Cribsheet** provides a massive ROI by cutting through the noise and saving you hundreds of hours of fruitless research. Furthermore, by making data-driven decisions on big-ticket items like childcare, you avoid the "trial and error" costs that can sink a family budget. This is the manual for the high-efficiency parent.
