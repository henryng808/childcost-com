Make Nice: Ryan Effgen on the Art of Conflict ROI

ChildCost Editorial Team
July 5, 2026 • 11 min read

Released in July 2026, Make Nice by Ryan Effgen is a razor-sharp exploration of how families navigate disagreement in an age of total transparency. Effgen provides a tactical manual for parents looking to turn family conflict into a source of growth rather than a drain on emotional resources.

The Economics of Agreement

Effgen identifies the "conflict liability"—the long-term cost of unresolved tension within the home. In the 2026 economy, where family stability is a primary hedge against external volatility, Make Nice acts as a manual for relational maintenance. The book emphasizes that a truthful, well-managed conflict has a higher long-term ROI than performative peace.

Strategic Lessons for Parents:

  • Relational Auditing: Identifying the invisible scripts that drive repetitive arguments.
  • Vulnerability as Asset: How sharing struggle builds child resilience and cognitive agency.
  • Systemic Forgiveness: Moving past errors to maintain the integrity of the family unit.

The ChildCost Verdict

Make Nice is the essential guide to family dynamics for the late 2020s. Effgen reminds us that the home is a sovereign zone where the currency is trust and the goal is connection.
