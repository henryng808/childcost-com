Dad, Love, Me: Matthew Quick's Letter to the Future

Released on July 1, 2026, Dad, Love, Me is a hauntingly beautiful memoir by bestselling author Matthew Quick. Written as a series of letters to his father, the book explores the raw emotional landscape of modern fatherhood, the weight of legacy, and the pursuit of connection in an increasingly fragmented world.

The Emotional ROI of Presence

Quick identifies the "invisible capital" of fatherhood—the moments of presence that compound over time to create a foundation of security for the next generation. His reflection on the struggle to maintain focus amidst the digital noise of 2026 is both a warning and a guide. The book serves as a roadmap for any father looking to build a legacy based on vulnerability and truth rather than just financial provision.

Core Principles for 2026 Dads:

  • Radical Honesty: The value of sharing our failures with our children to foster their own resilience.
  • Attention as Currency: Practical ways to reclaim focus and provide the undivided attention children crave.
  • Legacy Beyond Assets: Building an emotional inheritance that survives economic volatility.
