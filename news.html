The Silent Tax: Child Care Inflation Hits 5.2% as Wages Stagnate

New data reveals that child care inflation has decoupled from the general CPI, rising at nearly twice the rate of other essential goods. This "silent tax" is forcing parents to make impossible choices between career advancement and basic care.

According to the latest Bureau of Labor Statistics data, the cost of center-based care for infants has spiked by 5.2% over the last 12 months. In contrast, general consumer prices rose by only 2.8%. This widening gap represents a significant drain on household disposable income, particularly for millennial parents who are already battling student debt and housing affordability.

"We are seeing a structural shift where the cost of human labor in the care sector is becoming the primary driver of family poverty," notes senior economist Sarah Chen.
