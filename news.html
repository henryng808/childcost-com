World Bank Boosts 'Invest in Childcare' Initiative: New Targets Set for Global Affordability

On June 7, 2026, the World Bank convened a high-level stakeholder meeting to accelerate the "Invest in Childcare" initiative. The Bank announced new partnerships aimed at doubling global funding for childcare infrastructure in emerging markets by 2030.

The initiative highlights that for every $1 invested in quality childcare, there is a return of up to $4 in economic productivity and long-term developmental outcomes. The World Bank is now prioritizing "Integrated Care Hubs" that combine early education with maternal health services, specifically targeting regions where childcare costs exceed 20% of household income.

"Childcare is the ultimate economic multiplier. By scaling these investments, we are removing the single largest barrier to female workforce participation globally," said World Bank Director of Human Development.
