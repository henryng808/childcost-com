G7 'Care Workforce' Summit: Landmark Wage Floor Agreement to Combat Global Shortages

On July 14, 2026, labor ministers from G7 nations reached a historic agreement to establish a "Global Care Wage Floor," aiming to stabilize the childcare sector and attract millions of new workers to the industry.

The summit, held in Ottawa, addressed the critical "Care Workforce Gap" that has threatened to shutter 25% of providers across North America and Europe. The new framework includes a commitment to treat childcare workers as "essential economic infrastructure" and provides federal subsidies to ensure starting wages are at least 30% above local minimums. Analysts estimate this move will unlock $1.2 trillion in annual productivity by allowing more parents to return to the workforce with confidence.

"We are finally recognizing that the person caring for our children is the fundamental gear in the entire economic machine," says the G7 Secretariat for Labor.

NAFCC June 2026 Federal Policy Update: Stable Funding Secured for CCDBG

On June 29, 2026, the National Association for Family Child Care (NAFCC) released its mid-year federal policy briefing, confirming that Congress has successfully secured stable funding for the Child Care and Development Block Grant (CCDBG) for the remainder of the fiscal year.

The update provides a critical safety net as several states navigate the expiration of pandemic-era stabilization funds—a phenomenon often referred to as the 'Child Care Cliff.' By maintaining current funding levels and including modest increases for the PDG Birth to Five program, the federal government aims to prevent a projected 15% reduction in care capacity in high-demand regions. This stability is seen as a major victory for workforce retention in the post-AI transition economy.

"Stable childcare funding is the single most effective tool we have for economic resilience. Today's update ensures that millions of parents can remain in the workforce with confidence," says NAFCC Policy Director.
